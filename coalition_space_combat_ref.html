Your Turn
Move
Move up to your Speed in spaces. Split before/after actions.
Actions
Take 1 Full Action or 2 Quick Actions. Not both.
Reaction
1 per round, triggered by specific conditions. Resets at your turn.
Free Actions
Talk, drop items, activate toggles. GM discretion on quantity.
Full Actions  — uses your entire action
Barrage
Attack with 1 weapon from each of 2 different mounts, or once with a Superheavy. Each mount that fires may also fire an Aux weapon from that same mount — the Aux deals no bonus damage. You cannot fire two weapons from the same mount.
Stabilize
Choose one: cool all Heat (clears Exposed, Jammed, and 1 other condition) — or spend 1 Repair to restore HP equal to your Repair value.
Full Tech
Either use a system or protocol tagged Full Tech, or choose two Quick Tech options (the same option may be chosen twice).
Prepare
Declare a Quick Action. Trigger it as a reaction when a condition you name occurs.
Boot Up
Restart a Shut Down mech. Takes the full action.
Shut Down
Power off voluntarily. Clears all Heat. Mech is helpless until booted.
Quick Actions  — 2 per turn
Skirmish
Attack with 1 weapon. You may also fire a different Aux weapon on the same mount — that Aux weapon deals no bonus damage. Superheavy weapons cannot be used in a Skirmish.
Boost
Move again up to your Speed.
Lock On
Target gets the Lock On condition. It persists until consumed — any attacker may choose to consume it on their attack for +1 Accuracy. Once consumed, the condition ends.
Scan
Target must be within Sensors and line of sight. GM reveals one of: full statistics, one piece of hidden information, or one piece of general information about the target.
Hide
Requires cover or concealment. Become Hidden — position unknown until you attack or move into the open.
Quick Tech
Use a system or protocol tagged Quick Tech.
Reload
Reload a weapon with the Loading tag.
Overcharge
Gain a free Quick Action. Take Heat (see Overcharge table). Once per turn.
Reactions  — 1 per round
Brace
When hit: gain Resistance to all damage from that attack (other effects still apply). All other attacks against you gain +1 Difficulty until your next turn. Then: can only take 1 Quick Action on your next turn, no other actions, no reactions.
Overwatch
Trigger: a hostile character starts any movement within one of your weapons' Threat range. Immediately make a Skirmish against them before they move.
Systems
Some weapons and systems grant additional reactions. Check your mech sheet.
Making an Attack
1
Declare & Check Range
Target must be within weapon Range. For melee/CQB weapons, within Threat. Line of sight required unless noted.
2
Roll to Hit
Ranged / Melee: 1d20 + relevant stat vs. target Evasion
Tech attack: 1d20 + Tech Attack vs. target E-Defense
Accuracy: Roll extra d6, add highest result to d20
Difficulty: Roll extra d6, subtract highest result from d20
3
Determine Result
Miss: No effect (some weapons have miss effects)
Hit: Roll damage, subtract target's Armor
Critical (20+): Roll damage dice twice, take higher result — then add bonuses
4
Apply Damage & Effects
Damage reduces target HP. Armor reduces kinetic/energy/explosive damage only — not burn or tech. Apply any bonus effects from the weapon.

Conditions
Immobilized
Cannot move voluntarily. Can still act and attack.
Impaired
−1 Accuracy on all attacks, saves, and skill checks.
Slowed
Can only use your standard move — no Boost, no special movement from talents or systems. Actions are unaffected.
Stunned
No actions, no reactions, no movement. Evasion is capped at 5. Automatically fails all Hull and Agility checks and saves.
Shut Down
No actions. Evasion becomes 5. Immune to all tech actions. Clears all Heat, ends Exposed, ends statuses from tech actions.
Exposed
Take double kinetic, explosive, and energy damage. Does not affect Heat or Burn. Cleared by Stabilize or Shut Down.
Shredded
No longer benefits from Armor or Resistance. All damage applies fully.
Prone
You are Slowed. All attacks against you gain +1 Accuracy. Stand up: Quick Action.
Invisible
All attacks against you have a 50% chance to miss (roll 1d6: on 1–3, attack misses). You may always attempt to Hide. Cleared by attacking.
Hidden
Cannot be targeted by hostile actions. Does not cause Engagement. Enemies know only approximate location. Ends if you make any hostile action, reaction, Boost, or lose your concealment.
Lock On
Any attacker may choose to consume Lock On for +1 Accuracy on their attack. Persists until consumed — no round limit. Once consumed, the condition ends.

Repair & Rest
Stabilize
Full Action. Choose: cool all Heat (clears Exposed, Jammed, and 1 other condition — all three) OR spend 1 Repair to restore HP equal to your Repair value.
Repair (Rest)
Requires ~1 hour downtime. All Heat and conditions clear automatically. Then spend Repairs: 1 Repair = full HP restore OR repair a destroyed weapon/system. 2 Repairs = restore 1 Structure or 1 Stress.
Full Repair
Requires ~10 hours downtime. Restores full HP, Structure, Stress, and Repair Cap. Clears all conditions and statuses. Resets Overcharge counter. Recovers Core Power. Restores all Limited uses.
Structure
Structure damage is permanent until a Full Repair. It is a damage track (max 4) — at 4 structure hits the mech is destroyed. Costs 2 Repairs to remove 1 Structure during Rest.
Stress
Stress damage is permanent until a Full Repair. Each point of Stress remaining worsens future stress check outcomes — fewer remaining means worse meltdown results. At 4 stress: mech destroyed.
Things to Remember at the Table
Bonus damage
Bonus damage dice (from talents, systems, etc.) are rolled separately and added after the main roll. On a crit, bonus damage dice are also rolled twice.
Armor floor
Armor cannot reduce damage below 1 on a hit. You always take at least 1.
Crit = roll twice
On a crit, roll all damage dice twice and take the higher of the two rolls, then add flat bonuses once.
Reactions reset
You get 1 reaction per round. It resets at the start of your turn, not end of round.
Boost ≠ Move
Boost is a separate Quick Action that lets you move again — it's not part of your standard move.
Tech vs Evasion
Tech attacks target E-Defense, not Evasion. Check which your weapon uses.
HP ≠ Death
Hitting 0 HP triggers Structure damage — you're not dead yet. Your mech might be on fire, but it's still a mech.
1 reaction. Full stop.
You cannot Overwatch and Brace in the same round. You get one reaction per round — whichever you use first is the one you get. Choose carefully.
